How the City Budget Works

City vs. County — Two Separate Budgets

  • The City of Durham and Durham County are separate governments with separate budgets and separate tax rates. If you live in the city limits, you pay both.
  • The city tax rate funds police, fire, public works, water and sewer, parks, and planning. The county tax rate funds schools, health services, the sheriff, courts, and social services.
  • The FY2026 city tax rate was 55.77 cents per $100 of assessed value. The county rate was 73.57 cents. Durham residents within city limits pay both.
  • The City Manager presents a recommended budget each spring. City Council holds public hearings in May and adopts the budget by June 30.
  • Enterprise funds — water, sewer, solid waste — are funded by fees, not taxes, and are budgeted separately from the General Fund.
