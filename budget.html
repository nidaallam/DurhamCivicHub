Durham County · Fiscal Year 2026–27

Durham Budgets

Explore spending, revenue, and public participation opportunities for the county, city, and schools.

Durham County, the City of Durham, and Durham Public Schools each adopt separate annual budgets. Together they determine local tax rates, school funding, public safety staffing, housing programs, parks, and most of the services residents rely on every day.

🏛️
FY 2025–26 Adopted

Durham County

$689.7M

The county General Fund covers schools, health and human services, the sheriff, courts, and debt service. Property tax provides more than half of total revenue. The FY2027 proposed budget is $726.4M, with a public hearing on April 24.

FY 2025–26 Adopted

City of Durham

$835.7M

The city budget funds police, fire, public works, water and sewer, planning, parks, and transit. The city sets its own tax rate, separate from the county. City Council adopts the budget each June.

FY 2026–27 Requested

Durham Public Schools

$412.2M

DPS funding comes from three sources: the state (largest share), Durham County, and federal grants. The FY2027 county request is $412.2M — a 6.2% increase. A $7M+ state funding gap puts roughly 61 positions at risk.
