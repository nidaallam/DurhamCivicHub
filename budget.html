Durham County · FY2026–27 Budget Season

Durham Budgets

Official budget documents, the budget process, and how to participate — for the county, city, and schools.

Durham County, the City of Durham, and Durham Public Schools each adopt separate annual budgets. Together they determine local tax rates, school funding, public safety staffing, housing programs, parks, and most services residents rely on every day. All figures on this site are sourced directly from official government documents.

🏛️
FY2026–27 Process Underway — Manager Rec. Due May 19

Durham County

The county budget covers Durham Public Schools (largest share), Health and Human Services, the Sheriff, courts, parks, and libraries. Property tax provides the majority of revenue. The Board of Commissioners votes on adoption June 2.

Separate from Durham County

City of Durham

The city budget funds police, fire, public works, water and sewer, planning, parks, and transit. The city sets its own tax rate. Residents inside city limits pay both city and county taxes. City Council adopts the budget each June. The FY2025–26 adopted budget document is available from the Finance Department.

Durham Public Schools

DPS funding comes from three sources: the state (largest share), Durham County, and federal grants. Per the FY2026–27 recommended budget document, DPS requested $412.2M from the county and identified a $7M+ state funding gap putting roughly 61 positions at risk.
