About the Property Tax

Property tax is Durham County's single largest revenue source, funding 54% of the total budget. The current rate is 72.77 cents per $100 of assessed value.

Durham County conducts property revaluations periodically to ensure assessed values reflect market conditions. Commissioners set the tax rate each year during the budget process.

Property tax revenue grew by approximately $28M in FY2025 due to increased property values and modest development.

FY2025 Key Policy Investments

New and expanded initiatives in this year's budget
🏫
Education Investment
$407M total — Largest-ever county appropriation to Durham Public Schools. Includes 5% teacher pay increase, 12 new mental health counselors, pre-K expansion to serve 200 additional children, and $38M in capital repairs across aging school buildings.
🏠
Affordable Housing
$45M committed — Second-year drawdown of the 2022 $95M affordable housing bond. Funds 850 new affordable units, rental assistance for 1,200 households, and partnerships with Durham Community Land Trustees.
🧠
Mental Health & Crisis Response
$8.2M new funding — Countywide expansion of the co-responder crisis intervention program, 24/7 mobile crisis coverage, and 15 new behavioral health positions at Durham Center Access (the county's mental health authority).
🌳
Environment & Open Space
$52M total — Stormwater infrastructure, Ellerbe Creek restoration, expansion of the Durham open space program, and new funding for urban tree canopy initiatives in historically underinvested neighborhoods.
🚌
Transit & Mobility
$14M county contribution — Durham's share of the Durham-Orange Light Rail successor program and GoTriangle bus service expansion, including new routes serving East Durham and north Durham corridors.
⚖️
Equity & Opportunity
$6.5M new — Office of Equity & Inclusion initiatives, minority business development, summer youth employment program (500 slots), and cultural programs supporting Durham's diverse communities.

How to Weigh In on the Budget

The FY2026 budget process begins in spring 2025
📣
Speak at a Public Hearing
Budget public hearings are held each spring. Any resident can sign up to speak for 3 minutes before the Board of Commissioners votes.
✉️
Email the Commissioners
Written comments submitted to the Board before budget adoption are entered into the public record and reviewed by staff.
👀
Watch Work Sessions
Budget work sessions (where departments present to commissioners) are open to the public and live-streamed on Durham County's YouTube channel.
📋
Read the Manager's Proposal
The County Manager presents the proposed budget to commissioners each May. The full document is posted on the county website and available at all library branches.
