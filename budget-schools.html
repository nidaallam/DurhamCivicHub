How DPS Gets Funded

Durham Public Schools receives funding from three separate sources. Each has its own rules for what the money can pay for.

State of North Carolina
~61%
Largest single source
State funds pay for most teacher salaries, using a student allotment formula. The NC General Assembly sets the amount. When the state underfunds or cuts allocations, local taxpayers are often asked to make up the difference.
Durham County
$412.2M
FY2027 request · ~33% of total
County funds supplement state allocations and pay for many local priorities: additional teachers, mental health staff, facilities maintenance, and programs the state doesn't cover. The Board of Commissioners approves this amount each June.
Federal Grants
~6%
Title I, IDEA, and others
Federal funds primarily support students with disabilities (IDEA), low-income schools (Title I), and school nutrition. These grants are tied to specific uses and cannot replace state or county funding.
