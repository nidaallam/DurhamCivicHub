Durham County, NC

Civic Glossary

Plain-English definitions for the terms you'll hear at meetings, in budget documents, and across Durham government. No jargon required!

A

Absentee Ballot Voting
A ballot you fill out and submit before Election Day, either by mail or in person. In North Carolina, any registered voter can request one with no reason required.
Alliance Health Agency
The managed care organization that coordinates Medicaid-funded mental health, substance use, and developmental disability services for Durham County neighbors.
Appropriation Budget
The formal act by the Board of Commissioners or City Council to authorize spending from public funds. Durham's budget process starts with the County Manager's recommended budget each spring.
ARPA Agency
American Rescue Plan Act. Federal COVID-relief funds distributed to local governments starting in 2021. Durham used ARPA funds for housing, childcare, and infrastructure.
At-large Election Government
An election where candidates run countywide or citywide rather than from a specific district. All five Durham County Commissioners are elected at-large.

B

Board of County Commissioners (BOCC) Government
Durham County's five-member governing board. They set policy, adopt the budget, and oversee county services. Meetings are held at 200 E. Main St.
BRT (Bus Rapid Transit) Transit
A high-frequency bus service with dedicated lanes and off-board fare payment, similar to light rail but on rubber tires. GoTriangle proposed a Durham-Chapel Hill BRT line.

C

Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) Budget
A multi-year plan showing major infrastructure projects like road repairs, park upgrades, and building renovations that the county or city plans to fund. Durham's CIP covers 5 years.
CDBG Agency
Community Development Block Grant. Federal HUD funding that cities use for low-income neighborhood improvements, housing rehab, and public services.
Consent Agenda Government
A bundle of routine items voted on as a single block at the start of a board meeting. Any commissioner can pull an item for separate discussion.

D

Debt Service Budget
The annual payments Durham makes on bonds and loans it has taken out to fund capital projects. Roughly 11% of the county budget.
Department of Social Services (DSS) Agency
Durham County's agency providing food assistance (SNAP/EBT), Medicaid enrollment, Work First, child welfare, and crisis services.

E

Ellerbe Creek Transit / Development
A Durham watershed and trail corridor running through north Durham. The Ellerbe Creek Trail connects neighborhoods to Eno River parks.

F

Fiscal Year (FY) Budget
Durham's budget year runs July 1 through June 30. "FY2026" means July 1, 2025 through June 30, 2026.

G

General Fund Budget
The main operating account for the county or city, funded primarily by property taxes and sales taxes. Most day-to-day services are funded from the General Fund.
GoDurham Transit
The City of Durham's local bus network, operated by GoTriangle. Serves neighborhoods across the city with fixed routes and on-demand service.
GoTriangle Transit
The regional transit agency serving Durham, Chapel Hill, and the Research Triangle. Operates regional bus routes and paratransit services.

H

HEART Program Agency
Holistic Empathetic Assistance and Response Team. Durham City's program that sends mental health clinicians, not police, to non-violent behavioral health calls.

M

Millage Rate / Tax Rate Budget
The property tax rate, expressed as dollars per $100 of assessed value. Durham County's current rate is $0.5542 per $100.

O

Operating Budget Budget
The annual budget covering day-to-day expenses like salaries, utilities, and services. Different from the Capital budget, which funds physical infrastructure.

P

Precinct Voting
A geographic unit used to organize elections. Durham County has dozens of precincts, each assigned to a specific polling place.
Public Comment Government
The time during a meeting when community members can address the board. Most BOCC meetings allow 3 minutes per speaker, with sign-up required in advance.
Public Hearing Government
A formal meeting or meeting segment where the board is required by law to hear public input, often on zoning changes, the annual budget, or major projects.

Q

Quorum Government
The minimum number of members required to conduct official business. For Durham's 5-member BOCC, that's 3 members.

R

Rezoning Government
A formal request to change the allowed uses on a parcel of land. Rezonings require public notice, planning commission review, and a vote by city or county board.

S

Same-Day Registration Voting
In North Carolina, you can register to vote and cast a ballot on the same day during early voting, but not on Election Day itself.
SNAP/EBT Agency
Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. Food benefits issued on an Electronic Benefit Transfer card. Administered by Durham County DSS.

U

Unified Development Ordinance (UDO) Government
Durham's combined zoning, subdivision, and development regulations. It determines what can be built where in the city and county.

W

Ward Government
One of Durham's three city council districts (Wards 1, 2, and 3). City Council members represent these geographic areas plus mayor and at-large seats.
WIC Agency
Women, Infants, and Children. A federal nutrition program providing food vouchers and health support for pregnant women, new moms, and children under 5.
