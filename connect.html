Common questions

All five Durham County Commissioners represent the whole county (there are no districts). So I represent you! You can see the full board at dconc.gov/Board-of-Commissioners. If you want to know your City Council district (City of Durham is separate from the County), check the My Durham page.
Durham County has dozens of advisory boards and commissions that regular neighbors can join, from the Planning Commission to the Board of Health. Applications are open year-round at dconc.gov/government/advisory-boards. It's a great way to have a real impact! If you apply and want me to know, shoot me an email.
For most day-to-day service requests in Durham (potholes, trash, code violations, graffiti), call or use Durham One Call: 919-560-1200, or use the Durham One app. City services go through the City; County services (like DSS or Public Health) go through the County. Not sure who handles it? Email me and I'll point you in the right direction!
Anyone can speak during public comment! Sign up online or call (919) 560-0025 before 9 AM on meeting day. You get 3 minutes. Head over to the Meetings page for full details, sign-up links, and a comment helper to get your thoughts organized before you go.
Great question, it trips people up all the time! Durham County (run by the Board of Commissioners) handles things like social services, public health, elections, the Register of Deeds, and the county jail. The City of Durham (run by City Council) handles city police, city roads, water/sewer, parks, and planning within city limits. Some parts of Durham County are unincorporated and get county services but not city services. Most neighbors are served by both!
The official Durham County government site is dconc.gov. For the City of Durham, it's durhamnc.gov. Durham Civic Hub (this site!) links to official sources for everything but is my own community resource, not an official government site.
