About Durham's Elected Officials

Durham County Board of Commissioners — all 5 commissioners are elected at-large, meaning they represent the entire county, not individual districts. The Board sets county policy, approves the county budget, and oversees county departments including social services, public health, and the library system.

Durham City Council represents residents inside Durham city limits. The City Council sets city policy, the city budget, and oversees city services like police, parks, transit, and planning. If your address is in the unincorporated county (outside city limits), you don't have a City Council representative.

Durham City Council (durhamnc.gov) →

NC General Assembly. Durham is represented in both the NC House and NC Senate. Your specific district depends on your address. Representatives can be reached through the NC General Assembly website.

Find Your NC Legislators (ncleg.gov) →

Durham County Public Libraries are free for all Durham County residents! All branches offer books, e-books, computers, Wi-Fi, and community programs. A library card is free with proof of Durham County address.
