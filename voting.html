Voter ID Required in NC

North Carolina requires photo ID to vote. Here are the accepted forms. If you don't have one of these, you can still cast a provisional ballot and follow up within 9 days.

Always accepted
  • NC Driver's License or ID card
  • US Passport or passport card
  • Military ID
  • Tribal enrollment card
Also accepted
  • Out-of-state driver's license (if recently moved)
  • Student photo ID (NC college/university)
  • Government employee ID
  • Free NC voter photo ID card

No ID? Get a free NC voter photo ID card from the Durham County Board of Elections at no cost. Full ID rules at ncsbe.gov →

How to Vote

Early voting in Durham runs Oct 15–31, 2026 at multiple sites across the county. No excuse needed. Just show up with your ID at any early voting location in Durham County.

Same-day registration is available during early voting if you're not yet registered or need to update your address.

Find early voting sites →

On Election Day (November 3, 2026), polls are open 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM. You must vote at your assigned polling place, so look it up in advance. Bring your photo ID.

If you're in line at 7:30 PM, you have the right to vote. Don't leave!

Find your polling place →

Request an absentee ballot online and your ballot will be mailed to you. You need a witness to sign when you return it. Return by mail or drop it off at the Board of Elections by Election Day.

Request your ballot early! The deadline to request is typically a week before the election.
